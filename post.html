Interest in Finances

Interest: (n) A charge for borrowing money, typically expressed as a percentage of a principle amount borrowed. For lenders, it's the compensation for temporarily parting with their funds.
Compound vs Simple Interest: Simple interest grows at a constrant rate, based only on the original amount. Compound interest grows faster because interest earns additional interest over time. Even small rates, given enough years, can drastically increase savings or debt.

Professional Statement: Interest is your friend, not your foe.

Types of Interest

  1. Simple Interest
    • Based only on the principal amount
    • Grows linearly over time
  2. Compound Interest
    • Interest earns additional interest
    • Grows exponentially over time
